Claude Monet

Claude Monet, a pivotal figure in the Impressionist movement, revolutionized painting by focusing on capturing the fleeting effects of light and atmosphere, famously painting outdoors ("en plein air") with loose brushstrokes and vibrant colors to convey his immediate visual impressions. His iconic series paintings, such as Haystacks, Rouen Cathedral, and especially his later Water Lilies from his gardens at Giverny, exemplify his dedication to observing and depicting the subtle changes in nature, leaving an enduring legacy on modern art.
